Foods to Choose More Often

Choose More Often

Vegetables

Examples: broccoli, carrots, spinach, cucumbers, peppers, and more.

Vegetables

Why they are good: High in vitamins, minerals, and fibre. Canada’s food guidance recommends eating plenty of vegetables and fruits regularly.

Long-term effects: Supports digestion, heart health, and overall disease prevention.

Choose More Often

Fruits

Examples: apples, berries, bananas, oranges, and other fresh fruits.

Flip for benefits →

Fruits

Why they are good: Provide fibre, vitamins, and a naturally sweet snack choice.

Long-term effects: Can support heart health and lower the risk of chronic disease as part of a balanced diet.

Choose More Often

Whole Grains

Examples: oatmeal, brown rice, whole grain bread, whole wheat pasta.

Flip for benefits →

Whole Grains

Why they are good: Higher in fibre than refined grains, which helps with fullness and digestion.

Long-term effects: May help with blood sugar control, cholesterol, and weight management.

Choose More Often

Protein-Rich Foods

Examples: beans, lentils, eggs, fish, chicken, tofu, yogurt, nuts.

Flip for benefits →

Protein-Rich Foods

Why they are good: Canada’s food guide recommends choosing protein foods, especially plant-based options.

Long-term effects: Helps maintain muscle and supports overall health when prepared in healthier ways.

Foods to Eat Less Often

Eat Less Often

Sugary Drinks

Examples: pop, energy drinks, sweetened juice, bubble tea.

Sugary Drinks

Why they should be limited: These drinks are often high in sugar and low in nutrients.

Long-term effects: May increase the risk of weight gain, type 2 diabetes, and dental problems.

Eat Less Often

Examples: chips, candy, instant noodles.

Highly Processed Snack Foods

Why they should be limited: Often high in sodium, sugar, or saturated fat.

Long-term effects: May contribute to high blood pressure and weight gain.

Eat Less Often

Deep Fried Foods

Examples: fries, fried chicken, fried fast food.

Flip for reasons →

Deep Fried Foods

Why they should be limited: Often high in calories and saturated fat.

Long-term effects: May raise cholesterol and increase the risk of heart disease.

Eat Less Often

Foods High in Salt

Examples: processed meats, fast food, salty snacks.

Flip for reasons →

Foods High in Salt

Why they should be limited: Too much sodium can raise blood pressure.

Long-term effects: May lead to hypertension, heart disease, and stroke.

Easy Healthy Eating Reminders

  • Try filling half your plate with vegetables and fruits.
  • Choose water more often instead of sugary drinks.
  • Swap refined grains for whole grains when possible.
  • Pick protein foods that are baked, grilled, steamed, or lightly cooked instead of deep fried.
  • Enjoy treats sometimes, but aim for balance most of the time.
