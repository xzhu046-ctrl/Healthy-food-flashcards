Foods to Choose More Often

Vegetables

Long-term effects: Supports digestion, heart health, and overall disease prevention.

Fruits

Fruits

Sleep Apnea

Sleep apnea is more than just snoring. It can affect sleep quality, daytime energy, and overall health if it is not addressed.

Sleep Health

What Is Sleep Apnea?

A condition where breathing stops for short periods during sleep, sometimes many times in one night.

Tap or hover to flip →

What Is Sleep Apnea?

What it is: Sleep apnea is a condition where breathing repeatedly pauses during sleep. This can stop the body from getting enough restful sleep.

Why it matters: A person may sleep for many hours but still wake up feeling tired because their sleep keeps getting interrupted.

Sleep Health

Common Signs

Loud snoring, gasping, pauses in breathing, waking tired, morning headaches, and daytime sleepiness.

Tap or hover to flip →

Common Signs

  • Loud and frequent snoring
  • Pauses in breathing during sleep
  • Gasping, choking, or snorting at night
  • Waking up often
  • Morning headaches
  • Dry mouth or sore throat after waking
  • Feeling very tired during the day
Sleep Health

Why It Matters

Interrupted sleep can affect energy, mood, concentration, and overall health over time.

Tap or hover to flip →

Why It Matters

Important to know: Sleep apnea is more than a sleeping habit. When breathing is interrupted many times overnight, the body may not get enough oxygen and rest.

Possible effects: Low energy, poor focus, irritability, and increased risk of high blood pressure and heart problems.

Sleep Health

Helpful Next Steps

Medical follow-up, noticing symptoms, and supporting treatment without blame or shame.

Tap or hover to flip →

Helpful Next Steps

  • Speak with a healthcare provider
  • Ask whether a sleep assessment is needed
  • Keep track of signs the family notices at night
  • Support healthy sleep habits
  • Encourage follow-up if symptoms continue
  • Offer help in a respectful and non-judgmental way

Iron Deficiency

Iron deficiency can affect energy, growth, and overall health. Learning the common signs and ways to improve iron intake can help support the family’s well-being.

Iron Health

What Is Iron Deficiency?

A blood disorder that affects red blood cells when there is not enough iron in the blood.

Tap or hover to flip →

What Is Iron Deficiency?

What it is: Iron deficiency is a blood disorder that affects red blood cells and happens when there is a lack of iron in the blood.

Why it matters: Iron helps the body make healthy red blood cells that carry oxygen around the body.

Iron Health

Common Signs

Pale skin, fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, chills, cravings for ice, brittle nails, and hair loss.

Tap or hover to flip →

Common Signs

  • Pale or yellow skin
  • Constant fatigue and weakness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chills
  • Craving ice or non-food items
  • Brittle nails or hair loss
Iron Health

Why It Matters

Iron deficiency can affect thinking, growth, development, and daily energy.

Tap or hover to flip →

Why It Matters

Possible effects: Brain fog, tiredness, weakness, and growth or developmental delays in children.

Common cause: Iron deficiency is often caused by not getting enough iron from food or by not absorbing iron properly through the stomach.

Iron Health

Iron-Rich Foods & Treatment

Adding iron-rich foods and getting treatment when needed can help improve iron deficiency.

Tap or hover to flip →

Iron-Rich Foods & Treatment

Some iron-rich foods: quinoa, spinach, red meat, oysters, peanut butter, pumpkin or squash seeds, kidney beans, and lentils.

Treatment options: Healthcare providers may also recommend oral iron supplements or iron infusions.

Easy Healthy Eating Reminders

  • Try filling half your plate with vegetables and fruits.
  • Choose water more often instead of sugary drinks.
  • Swap refined grains for whole grains when possible.
  • Pick protein foods that are baked, grilled, steamed, or lightly cooked instead of deep fried.
  • Offer iron-rich foods regularly to help support healthy energy and growth.

Family Health Message

  • Healthy changes do not need to happen all at once. Small steps can still make a big difference.
  • The goal is not to remove all favourite foods, but to build a better balance over time.
  • If the family notices loud snoring, gasping, or breathing pauses during sleep, it is important to bring this up with a healthcare provider.
  • If there are signs of iron deficiency such as tiredness, pale skin, or weakness, medical follow-up may also be helpful.
  • Support works best when it feels respectful, encouraging, and free from blame.
